Introduction and Learning Objective

Introduction

The purpose of this image is to introduce students to the fundametal components of a computer. As we transition to talking about data types and how they interact with a computer it is essential students understand the basic components of a computer and microcontroller.

Learning Objective

Understand and describe the basic components of computers and micro-controllers

Design Elements

Contrast

The image employs contrast in each side of the image and is meant to either be a 2 sided page or a single image to keep the viewer focused on one side at a time. A 2-tone color scheme is employed to keep high contrast between elements and the image simple.

Repetition

The image repeats styling and placement of numbers and text from one element to another. Each side of the image is repeated to keep the visuals similar to one another.

Alignment

Numbers and text on the image are consistently aligned with eachother both within an individual side of the image as well as across the whole image. Each number and paragraph descriptor are aligned with eachother horizontally and vertically.

Proximity

Text and number headers are kept in close proximity to eachother and the image attempts to keep the viewer focsed on each side with the above elements without being too spread out.

Reflection

This project proved to be challenging for me. When I proposed my final project I was hoping to create digital media to support my students learning more about fundamental data types in Computer Science, however this was challenging to expand on in this project, because it would be extremely difficult for me to capture images of these data-types without highly advanced imaging devices available to see them in action on a stick of RAM. Due to this, I went with identifying the fundamental components of a computer, which is related and important for students and far easier to capture images of.

I also encountered challenges, as mentioned above, with the Adobe software not saving my PSD files correctly, which required me to re-do my work several times. I was also disconnected mid-session from the MU server when using that software, which meant I lost my work. There was a steep learning curve to the software, but it did become quite easier once I had figured out how to navigate the different tools.

Through the challenges I’m pleased with the final product and its appearance. I was able to apply different filtering techniques and am quite amazed at Photoshop’s ability to remove large items from and image and meld them into the background nearly seamlessly and also to isolate easily along edges. I wouldn’t call my final product comprehensive, in that it doesn’t explain how the individual components interact with each other. However, since most computers and microcontrollers share quite a few commonalities, it would help someone transfer their knowledge to identify basic components in a new system they would encounter.
